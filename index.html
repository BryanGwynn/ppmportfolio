Project Management Methodologies

Waterfall Methodology

Description: Waterfall is a linear, sequential approach where each project phase (such as requirements gathering, design, implementation, testing, and maintenance) must be completed before the next begins. This methodology is highly structured, with defined stages and milestones.

Agile Methodology

Description: Agile is an iterative and incremental approach that focuses on flexibility and customer collaboration. Agile projects are divided into small cycles called sprints, allowing teams to regularly review and refine project outcomes based on feedback. Agile frameworks like Scrum, Kanban, and Lean fall under this umbrella.

Kanban

Description: Kanban emphasizes visual task management using boards with cards and columns to represent work stages. Teams add, move, and complete tasks on the board, focusing on continuous delivery and improvement without fixed-length sprints.

Six Sigma

Description: Six Sigma aims to improve quality and reduce defects by using statistical analysis and process improvement techniques. Projects follow the DMAIC framework: Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, and Control. Six Sigma is data-driven and relies heavily on metrics.
