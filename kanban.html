Kanban Methodology

Kanban is a visual project management methodology designed to optimize workflows and reduce inefficiencies. Originally developed by Toyota for manufacturing, it has since been adapted to a wide range of industries, including technology and creative fields.

The core of Kanban lies in its visual board. Tasks are represented as cards and moved through stages like To Do, In Progress, and Done. This clear overview enables teams to identify bottlenecks and adjust workflows dynamically. Unlike other methodologies like Scrum, Kanban doesn’t enforce fixed-length sprints or designated roles. Instead, it promotes continuous delivery and flexibility—perfect for dynamic environments where priorities shift often.

Benefits of using Kanban:

  • Improved visibility with visual workflows
  • Enhanced flexibility and adaptability
  • Minimized waste and inefficiencies
